Online Magazine Outline

Body Paragraph 1

Topic sentence:

Excessive social media usage can result in drops in academic performance in various ways.

Supporting elaboration/explanation:

  1. Students will be easily distracted by social media (Dunn, 2011).
  2. Texting on social media will make pupil more regardless of grammar and sentence structures and rely more on online grammar checking tools (Hopkins, 2017).
  3. Nowadays, the Internet has almost made all information accessible. Thus, students will less likely to retain and remember the knowledge since they can check it out anytime on the Internet.

Examples:

  1. Pop-up messages from Facebook or WeChat grasp one's attention from his or her current task, making it harder to concentrate.
  2. Grammarly is a convenient way for students to check grammar mistakes. However, reliance on it make it harder for student to compose correct sentence in an exam since no computers or phones are allowed in that occasion.
  3. Take Quora as an example, once a specific question is typed in, the answer will come out within several milliseconds. Therefore, students feel unnecessary to emphasize on learning and retaining information.

Concluding statement:

In short, over-reliance on social media will contribute to poorer academic grades due to their distractions, regardlessness of grammar and convenience providing answers.

Source URLs:

  1. Hopkins, B. (2017). Impact of Social Media on Education. Retrieved from https://www.academiaapps.com/impact-social-media-education/
  2. Dunn, J. (2011). The 10 Best and Worst Ways Social Media Impacts Education. Retrieved from http://www.edudemic.com/social-media-education/
  3. Lad, H. (2017). The Positive and Negative Impact of Social Media on “Education, Teenagers, Business and Society”. Retrieved from https://www.ijirset.com/upload/2017/october/72_THE.pdf

Word Choices

Examples:

the most common mean of socializing is using applications designed for familiar individuals the most common mean of socializing is using applications designed for individuals who are familiar with each other
Singapore has kept a dense atmosphere of Chinese customs Singapore has kept a great atmosphere of Chinese customs

This is the most common error I made over the past few weeks. Paraphrasing is one of the most important skills to express ideas, since nobody is willing to read an article fulfilled with the same words. However, it will be foolish to use incorrect words or collocations that do not even exist. Thus, to ensure I have used the correct words or expressions that can clearly convey my opinions, I will search particular words or phrases I want to use and check out some examples. In this way, correct words will be used instead of rigid translation from Chinese.

Sticking to the topic

Examples: Monday paragraph writing
Though it is technically not a grammar mistake, it is a worth-mentioning critical mistake I make sometimes. I am currently a college student who strive hard to write academic articles. Thus, using references instead of my own opinions becomes extremely crucial. Nonetheless, essays in China are composed based on one's own knowledge and analysis. Thereby, I sometimes will stray away from my initial topic, the result of which is severe. Right now, I try to use examples I find to support my idea instead of my own "splendid theories"

Other grammar mistakes

There are of course other mistakes I made in assignments like tense and punctuation. However, the frequency is not so high, especially compared to mistakes on wrong word choices. Still, while improving my ability to choose corrects words that fit in with the context., I will stay cautious about these mistakes.

Compare and Contrast Essay

According to Merriam Webster, culture is the collection of traditions, values or social activities related to certain fields or particular societies. With the majority of citizens being Chinese, Singapore, a tiny yet developed country, has cultures blended with numerous Chinese elements. Meanwhile, being influenced by various ethnic groups, Singapore has found its own way of forming its unique culture with discrepancies between it and China.
While Singaporean and Chinese cultures both have acceptance of Chinese festivals, there are significant differences in terms of their time spent with families and senses of security.

To start with, Singapore has kept a great atmosphere of Chinese customs, especially Chinese festivals. For instance, during the most famous and momentous Chinese New Year Festival, Chinese will decorate their streets and houses for celebrating the occasion. Likewise, some lion dance troupes can be seen in Singapore with Singapore drums to accompany the performance (Tan, n.a). Additionally, the essence of the core values of Chinese festivals has not changed like family getting together on Mid-Autumn Festivals. This reflects both countries have respects to traditional Chinese festivals and their homogeneous inner cores. Thus it is not surprising for Chinese to find cultural affinity in Singapore (Charissa Yong, 2016). To sum up, Singapore has shown an amazing correspondence with China in celebrating Chinese festivals with acceptance of the internal values.

Nonetheless, although family reunion on festival has implied the great importance Singaporeans and Chinese attached to family, the ways and time they spent with families are not the same. According to a survey done by Channel NewsAsia’s (2016), the significance of family ranked highly among Singaporeans. However, Singapore is a developed country with intense competitions in companies and academies. Thereby to excel in academic or work fields, both students and employees should work hard to meet the requirements, the result of which is less time spent with family. Only half of the respondents said they spend more than 36 hours a week with their families according to Tho Ee Mine’s survey(2016). A typical phenomenon is Singaporeans tend to eat dinner separately, unlike Chinese eating together on one table as a family. Therefore, yet both Chinese and Singaporeans emphasize strongly on family, certain distinction exists in terms of time spent with family.

Apart from family time, another disparity between two countries’ cultures can be identified from senses of security. It is uncommon to see one serious case related to personal security in Singapore for the reason Singaporeans highly value safeness of themselves as well as others. In every aspect, safety is inviolable in Singapore. Hence Singaporeans would find it astonishing and sorrowful hearing the grievous news of Huang Na’s murder (Futt, 2013). Situations are different in China, where concepts of security in different regions vary a a lot with different education and economic conditions. Most of time, having a stable occupation to earn a living is the most critical security in a Chinese citizen's mind. Correspondingly, awareness of personal security is not as strong as Singaporeans. In short, senses of security differ greatly between Chinese and Singaporeans due to disparate concepts of security.

In conclusion, characteristic traits exist in both Chinese and Singaporean cultures. Some like festival celebrations are similar, some like family time and safety conceptions are not. There is no superior one between these two cultures since two countries all seek their own culture path towards a better society. Respect must be reserved regarding the resemblance and distinction so as to consider them objectively.

What I learned/Reflection

Why I chose this article:

Originally I saw the translated version of this article on a platform called "One Piece" which I found valuable. Thus I trace back and find the original version on The New York Times and chose it as the material for RLV Logs.

Summary of the articles:


As an incentive for human determination, convenience offers people with more efficient and relaxed method of coping tasks. Bearing "saving-the-efforts" in mind, humans are unconsciously driven by convenience. The revolution of convenience can be traced back in the 20th century when machine helped a lot in saving labor force. However some people resisted it because of the need of self-expression and thereby continued growing their vegetables. Then the second revolution which saw the potential of customization came. Numerous services for individualization came to the market, making self-expression inevitably homogeneous. Struggles may seem trivial in the era of convenience, but they provide individuals with the answer of who they are. In conclusion, while enjoying the convenience multiple tools bring about, one shall never forget the joy of doing something slowly yet happily in those "inconvenient" ways.
