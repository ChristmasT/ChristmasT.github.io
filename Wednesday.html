Individual Report of HL Project

Introduction

According to Henriques (2014), almost every college counseling center director reported that the number of students who have been diagnosed with mental illness is on the rise. This serious phenomenon has raised people's increasing awareness of students' mental health. Mental health is of crucial importance to a college student since college is the place where significant impacts on individuals take place. However, even if an undergraduate is in a terrible mental condition, little care is given to improve mental health, the result of which is undoubtedly slumps in academic performance (Gorczynski, 2018). Thereby, in order to have ideal grades and enjoyable life in universities, an undergraduate should give top priority to their mental health in universities (Wakeford, 2017). In this case, the most optimal mental state is positive attitude and peace of mind, which refers to a healthy mental state in which one is optimistic and willing to act towards solving problems without anxiety or negativity. In his article, Wakeford(2017) has identified academic stress, isolation, balancing work and study, financial difficulties and living independently as the main aspects of mental health. Nonetheless, things might be slightly different among NUS undergraduates. Aiming to find out the mental health conditions of NUS undergraduates, we had decided to investigate them through surveys. Through investigating about the factors causing happiness or stress and how our respondents cope with difficult situations in daily life, our group have got to know more about the general mental states of NUS undergraduates and some minor difference between students with different gender, faculty or grade.

Methodology

We had investigated 180 undergraduates in Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences(FASS), Faculty of Engineering(ENG), Faculty of Science(FoS), School of Design and Environment(SDE), School of Computing(SoC) and School of Business(BIZ). The researchers had been divided into 6 groups. Each group is responsible for surveying 30 students of one faculty through face-to-face interview or distributing questionnaires. The questions mainly focus on the origin of stress and happiness and the undergraduates' attitudes towards daily life.

8.15 Summary and Synthesis Assignment 1

Section A:

Lack of sleep can have various severe damage to human's body. Firstly, sleep deficiency can give rise to chaos of chemical distribution and further more to unstable mood. Secondly, short of sleep is part of reasons of workers' inattention which is the main cause of some tragic cases. Similarly, drivers are less likely to focus without sufficient sleep. Lastly, sleep absence caused by fanaticism or overloaded work may result in profound harm on health. Therefore excessive work and school plan should really be altered to ensure basic sleep requirement.

Section B:

Genetically modified food refer to the food whose genes have been changed by the scientists. When it comes to food with altered genes, people often have diverse opinions considering its effects. Firstly, transgenic food must has benefits since scientists develop them with positive intention. Those food can collect superior genes together to prevent themselves from being devastated by pests or herbicides (Food, Genetically Modified, 2014). The added genes can also stimulate the plants to have more nutrition, the result of which is better flavor and quality(Bosch, n.d). Moreover, it can help reserving the limited resource on earth such as water and energy(Bosch, n.d). Nevertheless, such highly controversial products indeed have some demerits. A plausible inference is variations caused by genes transformation might occur. Thus, concerned common sense no longer makes sense facing those food, especially to individuals who are allergic to certain type of food (Schneider and Schneider, 2009). Integration of two disparate comestible potentially results in severe consequences without mention of new constituent. More seriously, food engineers themselves might unconsciously compose some toxic chemicals during the process which certainly does massive harm to citizens' health (Vrain, 2013). In conclusion, while Genetically modified foods provide humans with foods with better resilience to possible damage and greater quality, they also give rise to worry about potential toxin and allergy it brings because of reform of genes and protein sequences.
