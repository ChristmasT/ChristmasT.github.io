What I learned/Reflection

(1) iPhone 3G: 10 Years Later

Why I chose this article:

I am a fan of technology. Therefore reading articles about technology will activate my motivation better compared to other articles.

Reflection:

This is actually a bit easy for me and the structure is apparent to see, although I can learn some words and sentences. Maybe next time I will try a more complex source of technology articles.

Summary of the articles:

10 years ago, an astounding work of art and technology came out: iPhone3G. This is a product added with loads of new functions while maintaining the acknowledged features of original iPhone. It provided customers with faster networking, more comfortable feel as well as a more reasonable price. These things together created a legend of selling 1 million iPhone within 1 week. And yet this is only the start of the Apple empire.

Words:

[The dictionary source are Eudic and Longman]

1.rear /rɪr/ adj
Def: at or near the back of something, especially a vehicle
Opp: front
Original sentence: The rear edges sloped more aggressively toward the screen, making the iPhone 3G feel thinner in the hand, despite being slightly thicker than the original.
Imitation: The newly released iPhone X's rear material change back to glass to enable wireless charging.

2.head-on /ˈhɛdˌɑn/ adv
Def: if someone deals with a problem head-on, they do not try to avoid it, but deal with it in a direct and determined way
Original sentence: Apple addressed this head-on.
Imitation: He asserted head-on that their price is fairly reasonable.

3.boast /bost/ v
Def: [intransitive, transitive] to talk too proudly about your abilities, achievements, or possessions
Original sentence: In his demos, Jobs boasted that 3G networking was 2-3x faster than EDGE, all of it with best-in-class battery life.
Imitation: Boasting in front of the public may not be a wise decision, try to keep low keyed.

Phrases and Expressions:

  1. stray far from
    Similar Expression: shift away from
    Original sentence: The iPhone 3G didn't stray far from the original design of the iPhone.
    Imitation: If you consistently hold a dream and never stray far from it., there won't anything stopping you from realizing it

  2. The biggest cheer regarding... came in regard to
    Similar Expression: The most acknowledge thing about ... is
    Original sentence: The biggest cheer regarding the design came in regards to the headphone jack.
    Imitation: The biggest cheer regarding iPad's innocation came in regards to 120Hz display.

(2) Taming the tiger mother (from The Economist)

Why I chose this article:

As I've mentioned before, the MacStories's article is a bit easy. Thus Economist seems to be a better choice.

Reflection:

The Economist has more complex words that can precisely convey the ideas. Additionally, their writers are all very sophisticated and their sentence structures are extremely natural. So, I think this is one of my greatest choices for English reading.

Summary of the articles:

Nowadays, students in China are suffering from a malformed education system which emphasize heavily on core subjects. Their parents, out of nervousness, will automatically send them to tutoring agencies for extra learnings. Chinese students may excel in tests like PISA, but that is the result of particular emphasis on subjects like math and science. Being exposed to such education system, students have two main problems: excessive homework burden and health issues. Therefore, Ministry of Education released its first "comprehensive learning assessment" to cope with current situation of lacking assessment on general abilities and pupils' overloaded work. This is a well-intentioned policy. However, it still hasn't touch the root of this phenomenon, namely, the fear of cruel Zhongkao Exam. The next step for Chinese education shall be how to solve that.

Words:

[The dictionary source are Eudic and Longman]

1.tame /teɪm/ v
Def: to reduce the power or strength of something and prevent it from causing trouble
Original sentence: Taming the tiger mothers
Imitation: We've successfully captured that bird, but how to tame it?

2.penultimate /peˈnʌltəmət, pə-/ adj
Def: not the last, but immediately before the last
SYN: last but one
Original sentence: The Ministry reckons that nearly one in ten pupils in the penultimate year of primary schools spend more than two hours of Chinese-language homework, every school day.
Imitation: Mom, you should be thankful that I am not the last one but penultimate one!

3.Shunt /ʃʌnt/ v
Def: to move someone or something to another place, especially in a way that seems unfair
Original sentence: The rest are shunted to vocational ones.
Imitation: It is sad to hear that my dearest friend was shunted to another school.

Phrases and Expressions:

  1. steamroller sb into
    Similar Expression: force sb to do sth
    Original sentence: and instead steamroller pupils into attending evening classes at tutoring centers where the teachers moonlight.
    Imitation: When I was a little boy, my mother always steamroller me into practicing the piano which turned out to be one of my best skills.

  2. crack down on
    Similar Expression: to impose sanction against
    Original sentence: The ministry is cracking down on rogue pedagogues who refuse to teach academic material in school.
    Imitation: President Xi had cracked down lots of corrupted officers in the government.
