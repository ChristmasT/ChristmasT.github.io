My English goals and study plan for Phase 1

Currently, I settle in a city pervaded with English and therefore not only daily communication, but also academic writing requires me to be proficient in English. My weakness is I sometimes will shift away from the central topic and my word usage is occasionally inaccurate. Thus writing and reading are the main focus for me in phase one. Of course, additionally I will continue practicing my listening and speaking to better fit in the environment. Here are my goals for English studying.

Goals:

  1. Capable of writing sophisticated academic essays. (better structures, word usage and sentence logic)
  2. Read more English books or articles to get familiar with some common words, phrases and structures. (Including but not limited to TED, Economist and book reading)
  3. Improve impromptu speech ability (watching speeches and seize every opportunity to present a speech)

Here are some of the measures I will take to make me excel in English.

  1. Through experience of mine, I found out that reading is the most efficient way to memorize words because I can naturally recall the sentence from which I can infer the meanings. Therefore I distribute some time for various English reading materials including Economist, tech articles (Macstories) and English books.
  2. Mere reading is not enough for sure, I need writing to consolidate the words or expressions I recently learn. So I will take English writing assignments seriously. If I have vacant time, I will compose one or two technology reviews and post that on my blog.
  3. In my opinion, listening and speaking are closely combined. Thus, I will watch some English videos to improve these abilities. As a matter of fact, my listening and speaking are dramatically improved via watching Youtube video without subtitles before I came to Singapore. Besides, I can learn certain speaking structures or words through watching English videos.

Of course, plans above might have minor changes according to the assignment or activities. However, the general goal stays the same: improving my English capability.
