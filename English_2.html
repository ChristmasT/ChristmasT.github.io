Adjusting to Singapore

A month! Without awareness of how time flies, I've spent a month in this city. Countless fresh new things emerged before my eyes within this month and I've strived hard to try to get used to them. Surprisingly, I can find similarity among most of unfamiliar features of Singapore: a sense of discipline.

Discipline doesn't necessarily have to be rules which must be strictly followed, but also united concepts or recognition planted in every citizen's mind. Queuing up consciously, lining up at the left hand side of escalators, banning drinking water on transportation means and be punctual are all representatives of obeying rules. It is because Singaporeans are willing to follow rules that they tend to be well-organized and efficient at dealing with tasks.

Being inspired by their rule consciousness, I gradually adopt some new methods for self scheduling. Except time for lectures and classes, SM2 scholars actually have plenty of time to arrange freely. Daily, weekly to monthly goals have been set. Thus simply following the plan is all I need to do, which is crucial to having sense of safety and clam coping upcoming events. After sticking to the plans for a few weeks, things I felt reluctant to do like working out become the default options for me. This is the magic of discipline.

Yet I cannot say I'm fully adjusted to Singapore lifestyle. As a matter of fact, I never will in this dynamic country. From glimpse of the breathtaking image of multitudinous cargo ships when I was in Labrador Park and the splendid scenery seen from Marina Bay Sands, I can sense the pulsation of this vigorous country. Beneath those spectacular landscapes, numerous opportunities for progress await.

China nowadays has increasingly significant status in the international network system. The most apparent reason is China is a country with 1.4 billion citizens. With the endeavor of government, there is a growing number of Chinese citizens who are connected to the Internet. Thus, generally speaking, I think a more positive future of Chinese Internet and its significant impacts on individuals can be sighted.
