Material:

The Economist 2018-09-21 (E-cigarettes are almost certainly better than smoking )

What I did

  1. Read through the articles.
  2. Get the main idea and basic structure.
  3. Read again to find out the new words.
  4. Compose a mind map.
  5. Upload new words to Anki (for future reviews).
  6. Try to imitate one or two sentences and use new words to write a sentence to consolidate them.

What I learned/Reflection

Outline

Words:

[The dictionary source is Longman]
1.endorse /ɪnˈdɔːs $ -ɔːrs/ v.
SYN:support
Def: to express formal support or approval for someone or something
Original sentence: There are plenty of officially _endorsed _methods for quitting.
Imitation: As a member of this party, I shall endorse whatever our leaders say, or otherwise I will be kicked out.

2.sanction /ˈsæŋkʃən/ n.
SYN: punishment
Def: a form of punishment that can be used if someone disobeys a rule or law
Original sentence: Earlier this month it put e-cigarette firms on notice that they must try to combat underage use of their products or face sanction.
Imitation: China had bravely fought back facing trade sanctions imposed by America.

3.skew /skjuː/ v.
Def: if something skews the results of a test etc, it affects them, making them incorrect
Original sentence: All sorts of confounding factors and overlooked connections can skew conclusions.
Imitation: Suddenly, lots of animals rushed in, skewing the figures we are studying on.

Phrase

1.borne out
SYN: support
Def: if facts or information bear out a claim, story, opinion etc, they help to prove that it is true
Original sentence: But, says Dr West, these concerns have not yet been borne out by epidemiological studies.
Imitation: Although numerous people claimed they had prove the Riemann Hypothesis, none of the proof of whom have been borne out valid.

Sentence

Translation: 尽管含量少到非常不明显，一种致癌物亚硝胺仍然在电子烟中被发现。
