Material:

The Tyranny of Convenience- The New York Times

URL:
https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/16/opinion/sunday/tyranny-convenience.html

What I did

  1. Read through the articles.
  2. Get the main idea and basic structure.
  3. Read again to find out the new words.
  4. Compose a mind map.
  5. Write a brief summary.
  6. Upload new words to Anki (for future reviews).

What I learned/Reflection

Why I chose this article:

Originally I saw the translated version of this article on a platform called "One Piece" which I found valuable. Thus I trace back and find the original version on The New York Times and chose it as the material for RLV Logs.

Summary of the articles:


As an incentive for human determination, convenience offers people with more efficient and relaxed method of coping tasks. Bearing "saving-the-efforts" in mind, humans are unconsciously driven by convenience. The revolution of convenience can be traced back in the 20th century when machine helped a lot in saving labor force. However some people resisted it because of the need of self-expression and thereby continued growing their vegetables. Then the second revolution which saw the potential of customization came. Numerous services for individualization came to the market, making self-expression inevitably homogeneous. Struggles may seem trivial in the era of convenience, but they provide individuals with the answer of who they are. In conclusion, while enjoying the convenience multiple tools bring about, one shall never forget the joy of doing something slowly yet happily in those "inconvenient" ways.

Words:

[The dictionary source are Eudic and Longman]
1.beget /bɪˈɡet/ v
Def: to cause something or make it happen
SYN: produce
Original sentence: Yet our taste for convenience begets more convenience, through a combination of the economics of scale and the power of habit.
Imitation: It is quite natural to have transformations on teenagers' body since it is the phase of time when hormones beget growth of physical bodies.

2.onerous /ˈɒnərəs, ˈəʊ- $ ˈɑː-, ˈoʊ-/ adj

Imitation: Although English assignments may seem onerous, they are extremely helpful in making progress in English :).

3.perverse /pəˈvɜːs $ pərˈvɜːrs/ adj

SYN: bizarre

Imitation: What is perverse is that when Apple released the iPhone X, numerous people felt "disgusted" seeing the notch. But later, they accepted that silently and start using upcoming imitators of that masterpiece.

Def: formal to give someone the political or legal rights that they did not have before
SYN: free

RLV Log2

Material:

TED- The rapid growth of the Chines internet and where it is headed

URL: https://www.ted.com/talks/gary_liu_the_rapid_growth_of_the_chinese_internet_and_where_it_s_headed/transcript?

What I did:

  1. Listen to the speech three times with different purposes.
  2. Find out the new words and make them into Anki cards.
  3. Map out the mind map
  4. Listen again and find out great sentences.
  5. Compose sentences using new words.
  6. Write a summary
  7. Write a review.

Summary:

Chinese Internet market has unimaginable potential waiting to discover due to its massive number of population and governments supports. However, beside economic effects, the Internet can also bring prominent social impacts in two ways. Firstly, the booming of Internet creates millions of jobs that can't be even imagined before thanks to the fast and vast connection across the country provided by webs. Additionally, network makes online education easier than ever before. Thus students from rural areas can also witness the splendid word and be educated by great teachers. In conclusion, the growing network infrastructure construction has really benefit billions of citizens which incents us to continue putting in capital and effort to make it step further.

Words:

  1. incentive /ɪnˈsentɪv/ n
    Origin: The world's largest pool of tech talent exists because of the abundance of educational incentives.
    Def: something that encourages you to work harder, start a new activity etc
    Example: As an added incentive, there’s a bottle of champagne for the best team.
    Synonym: motivation
    Sentence Imitation: Sometimes a warm coffee or a snug hug is the best incentive even compared to money.

  2. censorship /ˈsensəʃɪp $ -ər-/ n
    Origin: Of course you cannot observe the Chinese internet without widespread censorship and very serious concerns about dystopian monitoring.
    Def: the practice or system of censoring something
    Sentence Imitation: Without censorship from teachers or parents, students lack self-control will easily get lost in the virtual world of games.

  3. irrevocable /ɪˈrevəkəbəl/ adj Origin: As the Chinese internet continues to grow even in its imperfection and restrictions and controls, the lives of its once-forgotten populations have been irrevocably elevated
    Def: an irrevocable decision, action etc cannot be changed or stopped
    Sentence Imitation: Come on! Since you are going to working out, soaking your T-shirt is irrevocable!

    Sentences:

1.It is an overwhelming need economy that is serving an underprivileged population, which has been separated for 30 years from China's economic boom.

Translation: 这是一个为弱势群体服务的巨大需求经济，却被中国经济繁荣的30年排除在外。

2.Wherever this alternative fuel exists, be it in China or Africa, Southeast Asia or American heartland. We should endeavor to follow it with capital and with effort, driving both economic and societal impact all over the world.

Translation: 无论这种替代方式存在于何处，在中国或者非洲，东南亚或者美国心脏地带。我们应该用资本和努力来实现这一目标，驱动全球经济和社会影响。

Review and thinking:

China nowadays has increasingly significant status in the international network system. The most apparent reason is China is a country with 1.4 billion citizens. Therefore, even a tiny origin can stir a mass disturbance on the Internet which can be either positive or negative. Thus countless opportunities can be found in the phase when China is developing. However, instead of merely emphasize on how enormous Chinese Internet marketplace is. The speech stressed more on the facility the Internet can offer to those impecunious families with more alternatives. The Internet is offering billions of people with plentiful work positions as well as more advanced education within the speed of life. For those who came from metropolises, accessing to the Internet may be a rather easy task. Nevertheless, their ease may never be reachable for unprivileged individuals, speaking of which Chinese government's effort must be noticed. Their controversial policy of manipulating the overall internet environment definitely will have negative impacts on certain developments of the network. But on the other hand, the special policy also prevents Chinese companies being crushed by international network companies which can subsequently lead to better and more stable occupations offered to people, especially those from poor backgrounds. Moreover, since the Chinese government has so much power compared to another country, it can implement massive measures to optimize the Internet infrastructures. With the endeavor of government, there is a growing number of Chinese citizens who are connected to the Internet. Thus, generally speaking, I think a more positive future of Chinese Internet and its significant impacts on individuals can be sighted.

What I learned/Reflection

(1) iPhone 3G: 10 Years Later

Why I chose this article:

I am a fan of technology. Therefore reading articles about technology will activate my motivation better compared to other articles.

Reflection:

This is actually a bit easy for me and the structure is apparent to see, although I can learn some words and sentences. Maybe next time I will try a more complex source of technology articles.

Summary of the articles:

10 years ago, an astounding work of art and technology came out: iPhone3G. This is a product added with loads of new functions while maintaining the acknowledged features of original iPhone. It provided customers with faster networking, more comfortable feel as well as a more reasonable price. These things together created a legend of selling 1 million iPhone within 1 week. And yet this is only the start of the Apple empire.

Words:

[The dictionary source are Eudic and Longman]

1.rear /rɪr/ adj
Def: at or near the back of something, especially a vehicle
Opp: front
Original sentence: The rear edges sloped more aggressively toward the screen, making the iPhone 3G feel thinner in the hand, despite being slightly thicker than the original.
Imitation: The newly released iPhone X's rear material change back to glass to enable wireless charging.

2.head-on /ˈhɛdˌɑn/ adv
Def: if someone deals with a problem head-on, they do not try to avoid it, but deal with it in a direct and determined way
Original sentence: Apple addressed this head-on.
Imitation: He asserted head-on that their price is fairly reasonable.

3.boast /bost/ v
Def: [intransitive, transitive] to talk too proudly about your abilities, achievements, or possessions
Original sentence: In his demos, Jobs boasted that 3G networking was 2-3x faster than EDGE, all of it with best-in-class battery life.
Imitation: Boasting in front of the public may not be a wise decision, try to keep low keyed.

(2) Taming the tiger mother (from The Economist)

Why I chose this article:

As I've mentioned before, the MacStories's article is a bit easy. Thus Economist seems to be a better choice.

Reflection:

The Economist has more complex words that can precisely convey the ideas. Additionally, their writers are all very sophisticated and their sentence structures are extremely natural. So, I think this is one of my greatest choices for English reading.

Summary of the articles:

Nowadays, students in China are suffering from a malformed education system which emphasize heavily on core subjects. Their parents, out of nervousness, will automatically send them to tutoring agencies for extra learnings. Chinese students may excel in tests like PISA, but that is the result of particular emphasis on subjects like math and science. Being exposed to such education system, students have two main problems: excessive homework burden and health issues. Therefore, Ministry of Education released its first "comprehensive learning assessment" to cope with current situation of lacking assessment on general abilities and pupils' overloaded work. This is a well-intentioned policy. However, it still hasn't touch the root of this phenomenon, namely, the fear of cruel Zhongkao Exam. The next step for Chinese education shall be how to solve that.

Words:

[The dictionary source are Eudic and Longman]

1.tame /teɪm/ v
Def: to reduce the power or strength of something and prevent it from causing trouble
Original sentence: Taming the tiger mothers
Imitation: We've successfully captured that bird, but how to tame it?

2.penultimate /peˈnʌltəmət, pə-/ adj
Def: not the last, but immediately before the last
SYN: last but one
Original sentence: The Ministry reckons that nearly one in ten pupils in the penultimate year of primary schools spend more than two hours of Chinese-language homework, every school day.
Imitation: Mom, you should be thankful that I am not the last one but penultimate one!

3.Shunt /ʃʌnt/ v
Def: to move someone or something to another place, especially in a way that seems unfair
Original sentence: The rest are shunted to vocational ones.
Imitation: It is sad to hear that my dearest friend was shunted to another school.

Phrases and Expressions:

  1. steamroller sb into
    Similar Expression: force sb to do sth
    Original sentence: and instead steamroller pupils into attending evening classes at tutoring centers where the teachers moonlight.
    Imitation: When I was a little boy, my mother always steamroller me into practicing the piano which turned out to be one of my best skills.

  2. crack down on
    Similar Expression: to impose sanction against
    Original sentence: The ministry is cracking down on rogue pedagogues who refuse to teach academic material in school.
    Imitation: President Xi had cracked down lots of corrupted officers in the government.
